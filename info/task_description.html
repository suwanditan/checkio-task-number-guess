Given an unknown number, range from 1 to 100, and your task is, guess the number by doing series of attempts.

Your solution will guess the number by submitting an integer divisor, range from 2 to 10, and your guess number. At each attempt, you will get information, as a list of tuples, whose each tuple contains its remainder result, and your previous divisor.

You should find the number in 8 guesses. And, you've got extra one info in your first attempt. Use it wisely.

Consider this following example:
Attempt Divisor Guess Submit Information Remark
1 2 99 (2,99) (1,5),(1,2) You try a divisor 2, and try your luck with 99. However, you've got information a list of tuples, returning remainder of your previous divisor, and your previous divisor. ( that's mean, you still need to guess )
2 3 91 (3,91) (1,5),(1,2),(2,3) Your solution may suggest 91 because it passed with the information data, isn't it? ( 91%5=1; 91%2=1 ). However, again, you've still got response. Next guess, then.
3 6 41 (6,41) (1,5),(1,2),(2,3),(5,6) Your solution may suggest 41 because it passed with the information data, isn't it? ( 41%5=1; 41%2=1; 41%3=2 ). However, again, you've still got response.
4 4 71 (4,71) (1,5),(1,2),(2,3),(5,6),(3,4) Your solution may suggest 71 because it passed with the information data, isn't it? ( 71%5=1; 71%2=1; 71%3=2; 71%6=5 ). Now, remaining 4 attempts only.

Input: Information about previous attempt. A list of tuples. Each tuple contains its remainder and previous divisor.

Output: A list of integer divisor and your best guess. A list of two integers.

Example: 

checkio([(1,5)])                            # the number has a remainder 1
checkio([(1,5),(1,2)])                      # the number has a remainder 1
checkio([(1,5),(1,2),(2,3)])                # the number has a remainder 2
checkio([(1,5),(1,2),(2,3),(5,6)])          # the number has a remainder 5
checkio([(1,5),(1,2),(2,3),(5,6),(3,4)])    # the number has a remainder 3
